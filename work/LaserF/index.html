LaserF Soccer Goal

The LaserF soccer goal was a semester-long independent study project completed with a team of 4 undergraduate engineers and 6 Engineering Management Graduate Students. With a prompt of “reimagining fitness,” our team changed the game of soccer to quantify the locations of made shots.

LaserF Soccer Goal
From Ideation...

Working with the graduate student managers was a rewarding experience that challenged me to articulate ideas to teammates and managers alike. I used this ideation page to help convince the managers to pivot the product vision from tennis to soccer.

ideation.jpg
To Early Prototyping...

The team decided to use an adjustable laser-target system using laser diodes and photoresistors. To add robustness to a range of ambient light situations, we used trimpots to adjust readings from the photoresistor “targets.”

lasers_targets.jpg
To a viable product!

With a Python GUI, we were able to take Arduino data of what lasers were broken to compute where the ball went and display it on a connected laptop.
