Tenting Optimization Program
The Problem

very Spring, the first day of classes is accompanied by the start of the most wonderful time of the year- tenting season. In order to get a seat in Cameron Indoor for the Duke-UNC game, students take shifts in line starting in January up until the game, whenever it ultimately is. At all hours of the day, 2 members of 12-person tents must be present. At night, 10 members are required. Tent checks can be called 24/7.

Picking and optimizing schedules is one of the most tedious aspects of the tenting process- I wanted to make it easier on my team so they could focus on the important things- namely, booing UNC.

Tenting.jpg
The Approach

In order to make tent scheduling easier, I'm developing a program that reads an Excel sheet of the team's individual availabilities and goes through the entire week in half-hour blocks, assigning shifts based on a combination of availability and 'tenter score.' This score quantifies tent involvement based on day shifts, night shifts, and spirit event participation- lower scored members are given higher priority on shifts.

Loser has to take the SAT at Shooters II Nightclub. For real.
