ShotBot

Making use of an ultrasonic sensor and a peristaltic pump, the ShotBot is capable of enhancing any responsibly enjoyed occasion. It dispenses an internally contained liquid until it detects that the detected height has increased by 2 cm.

ShotBot Complete.jpg
What's going on inside?

Early iterations using a push-button sensor were quickly deemed clunky and inefficient. The ultrasonic sensor simplified the UI and can quantify a responsible pour.

ShotBot Internals.jpg
And outside?

The casing was laser cut into 1/8" wood and utilized a custom finger joint design. Gatsby making a toast was a final detail.

ShotBot Case.jpg
Mounting the sensor

Keeping the ultrasonic sensor at a constant height was a challenge. Flush mounting a custom print (with a hole for the pump) allowed me to keep it rigidly mounted, albeit with only a friction fit for easy troubleshooting.

Sensor Mount.jpg
Future iterations

Future iterations will place the tube slightly further away, as at times it interferes with distance readings.
